Vinyl Wrap

What is Vinyl Wrap ?

Vinyl wrapping involves adding a slim vinyl layer onto the exterior of a vehicle, with the goal of safeguarding the paint or altering the appearance. Another common use of vinyl wrapping is for decorating the exterior of vehicles with custom designs, company logos, or commercial advertising, which can be seen on both private and commercial vehicles.

The Hamilton Area, Ontario, and Canada as a whole have seen a surge in the popularity of vehicle wrapping in recent years. Wrapping your car in vinyl not only alters its appearance, it also shields its paint from the harsh winter weather in Hamilton.

COLOR CHANGE VINYL WRAPS

In recent years, vehicle wrapping has gained popularity in the Hamilton area, Ontario, and throughout Canada. This method of covering your car with vinyl not only changes its appearance but also provides protection against the severe winter weather in Hamilton.

One of the benefits of vinyl wrapping is that it allows you to easily remove it whenever you desire. Whether you no longer enjoy the color or plan to sell the car, simply taking off the vinyl will restore the paint to its original condition. Additionally, this technique isn't exclusive to new cars. If you've discovered a secondhand vehicle that's perfect in every aspect except for its paint job, we can make it look brand new with vinyl wrapping.

How much does it cost ?

The expense of a car wrap is determined by several factors, including the car model, the caliber of the wrapping material, and whether it's a full or partial wrap.

In general, a full car wrap for a private vehicle can cost between $3,500 to $5,500, while a commercial truck wrap can range from $3,000 to $5,000.

CAN A VINYL WRAP BE REMOVED ?

Yes, a vinyl wrap can be easily removed if high-quality film was used during the installation process. You can use a heat gun to remove the wrap yourself or take it to a professional detailing studio in Hamilton.

The cost of removing a car wrap typically ranges up to $1000.

HOW LONG DOES A VINYL WRAP LAST ?

The lifespan of a vinyl car wrap can be up to seven years, depending on the quality and thickness of the vinyl film used. A thinner vinyl film may last only 4 to 5 years.

CAN A WRAPPED CAR BE WASHED ?

Yes, but not for two weeks following the wrapping process to ensure proper adhesion of the new vinyl surface. After that, you can wash your car as usual, although hand cleaning is recommended for the best durability of the car wrap. Automated car wash brushes can be abrasive and cause the wrap layer to peel and lift prematurely, resulting in the film edges peeling and lifting. However, using a car wash that only uses water sprays, without brushes, is an exception to this rule.
